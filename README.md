Cervixity
An agent-based model (ABM) simulating cervical cancer screening behaviour among women living with HIV in underserved communities, built with NetLogo.

Overview
Cervical cancer disproportionately affects women in low-resource settings, particularly those living with HIV. This model simulates how individual and community-level factors influence whether women seek cervical cancer screening - with the goal of identifying interventions that could improve uptake.

How It Works
Agents represent women with attributes including:
Language preference — reflecting communication accessibility
Knowledge level — awareness of cervical cancer and screening
Healthcare trust — willingness to engage with health systems

The simulation models how these attributes interact over time to influence screening behaviour across a population.
Key Finding
Results demonstrated that localized AI communication - messages tailored to language and knowledge level — significantly improves health-seeking behaviour. This supports evidence-based design of community health interventions.
